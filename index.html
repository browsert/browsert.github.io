REGIOMONTANUS' ANGLE MAXIMIZATION PROMLEM

Oscar Hubble

Abstract. This is a solved walkthrough of a famous optimization problem. This proof lies on elementary geometry concepts already documented in Euclid’s Elements. The problem goes like this: A Spacecraft flies through the solar system in what can be considered a straight line. Given the distance earth-sun, at what point of the spacecraft’s trajectory should the occupants perceive the biggest angle earth-sun. The objective is to maximize the angle subtended by the segment earth-sun and whose vertex is at the occupant’s eye.

I urge the reader to think the problem threw before the explanation is laid. Every statement and principle can be derived after some thought. And even If, the solution sinks better if you have struggled.

1. FINDING THE OPTIMIZED ANGLE

{1} The locus of all equal angles subtended by the same fixed segment is a double arc. This derives from the inscribed angle theorem which states that an angle \alpha inscribed in a circle is half the central angle 2\alpha that subtends the same chord on the circle. The radius of the arc is inversely proportional to the inscribed angle it houses. The objective is to find the smallest circle tangent to the flight path h and passing through A & B. The centre of the circle e lies on the perpendicular bisector g formed by A & B. The radius of the circle is the distance j from C to E.
